New Zealand's Upcoming Events

3rd March 2017

You can’t go past Kellogs Nutri-Grain IRONMAN New Zealand as the elite event of New Zealand endurance sport. 3.8km fresh water swim, 180km cycle and 42.2km run.

Ironman New Zealand

Firmly established in its hometown of Taupo since 1999, IRONMAN New Zealand showcases the best of New Zealand's outdoors and its breathtaking scenery. Thanks to the 'pure Kiwi spirit', you will feel like a local the minute you arrive. The Taupo event attracts more than 2,000 volunteers from a population of just 22,000—and you'll encounter the rest along the streets cheering you on.

Triathlon New Zealand has confirmed that the race on Saturday 3 March will be the official New Zealand Ultra Distance Championships, which is open to all New Zealand citizens and/or have permanent resident status. New Zealand also offer 40 qualifying slots for the IRONMAN World Championship in Kailua-Kona, Hawaii.

See full race details at Ironman.com.

April 2017

The World Masters Games is the world’s largest multi-sport event. Held every four years, it is the pinnacle sporting event for masters competitors worldwide.

World Masters Games

World Masters Games 2017 is hitting Auckland and the Waikato from 21-30 April 2017, with 48 competition venues hosting the action.

In supporting the Olympic Games ethos of ‘sport for all’, the goal of the World Masters Games is to encourage participation in sport throughout life.

See full World Masters Games schedule and venue details.

New Zealand Lions Tour

The eagerly anticipated tour by the British and Irish Lions will provide fans with a feast of quality rugby which will be LIVE on SKY Sport.

The Lions, who last toured New Zealand in 2005 will be chasing their first series win against the All Blacks since their historic triumph in 1971.

Rugby fans are in for a treat with the Lions kicking off the tour in Whangarei against the New Zealand Barbarians, followed by games against all the Super Rugby teams, the Maori All Blacks and a three test match series.

14th - 17th June 2017

Fieldays is the Southern Hemisphere's largest agribusiness exhibition - to be held 14 to 17 June 2017 at Mystery Creek Events Centre, Hamilton.

Fieldays

Fieldays is the Southern Hemisphere's largest agribusiness exhibition. Experiencing everything from the latest technological advancements and Kiwi ingenuity in the Innovations Centre, to watching the Rural Bachelors demonstrate their agricultural expertise, to picking up the best deal on the latest farm equipment, you can experience it all at Fieldays 2017.

NZ Agricultural Fieldays 2017 will be held 14 to 17 June 2017 at Mystery Creek Events Centre, Hamilton. Find out more by visiting the Fieldays website.

22nd - 25th June

A four day extravaganza of Queenstown's unique culture and community with street parties, fireworks, international and local acts, comedy, family fun, and plenty of Mountain Mayhem.

Queenstown Winter Festival

The official celebration to mark the beginning of Queenstown’s winter season. Town will be a-buzz with people celebrating the arrival of winter in true Queenstown style.

The Queenstown Winter Festival will be jam-packed with fantastic events across Thursday 22 June - Sunday 25 June. A four day extrazaganza celebrating the start of winter like only Queenstown can.

Check out Queenstown Winter Festival for more details.

Aug & Sept 2017

Winter Olympic, Paralympic and X Games stars are heading to New Zealand’s Southern Alps once again for the first major championships of the season.

Winter Games New Zealand

The fifth biennial Audi quattro Winter Games NZ are taking place during August and September 2017. Watch the worlds best compete for glory in freestyle skiing, snowboarding, alpine skiing, cross-country skiing and curling, then enjoy the fun festival programme that promises to be bigger and better than ever.

The Games are one of the world’s top five winter sports competitions and the only one in the southern hemisphere.

For the latest event news and updates got to Winter Games NZ.

Oct - Dec 2017

Can Australia defend their title and maintain their world dominance in Rugby League? We'll see in this year's Rugby League world cup, hosted in Australia, New Zealand and Papua New Guinea.

Rugby League World Cup

The Rugby League World Cup is Rugby League’s premier international competition. First held in 1954, the 2017 Rugby League World Cup is the 15th iteration of the historic tournament. The 2017 competition will feature 14 mens and 6 womens teams from around the globe, hosted in Australia, New Zealand and Papua New Guinea.

Go to Rugby League World Cup 2017 for full schedule and ticketing

Racing and fashion go hand-in-hand and there is no time where style is more at the forefront than during New Zealand Cup and Show Week.

NZ Cup & Show Week

Hold onto your hats fashionistas – New Zealand Cup and Show Week is your time to shine.

New Zealand Cup and Show Week will be held in Christchurch from Saturday 11 to Saturday 18 November 2017. Christchurch is vibrant with retail areas throughout the city and the night life is buzzing.

Riccarton Racecourse and Addington Raceway will be awash with fabulous fashions so if you don’t want to be lagging behind the competition you need to hit the stores now and start piecing together your race-day outfit.

See full race day details at New Zealand Cup & Show Week.

More Than Just The Outdoors

Although justly famous for action and adventure, New Zealand boasts a wide range of other activities and festivals to keep you entertained from dawn till dusk - and beyond!

New Zealanders love to celebrate, and you’ll find all manner of parties and festivals happening throughout the year. Wine, music, arts and crafts, sports, horse racing, dance and fashion, flowers, architecture, scallops and even Huhu Grubs take their turn. New Zealand loves to highlight its natural talents and bounty! So whether you are a fashionista, want to get a dose of Kiwi live music or sample colourful New Zealand cuisine, ensure you catch some of the country’s classic events.

Sample fresh local produce and world-class wine at one of the many food and wine festivals. Go on an adventure through lush countryside to majestic mountains on a cycling event. Share the thrill of being in the audience of a major sport event or take part in a race yourself. Experience New Zealand’s native culture and thriving arts scene or enjoy an outdoor music festival set against a beautiful backdrop. Can't find what you're looking for? Check out Eventfinda for a full list of local and community based events.
